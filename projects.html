Research Projects

GPAM is involved in funded research projects spanning Automated Machine Learning, data streams, evolutionary computation, neural architecture search, and large-scale collaborative AI initiatives with social and environmental impact.

AutoML for Data Streams

Period: 2026 - Present

Status: Ongoing

Funding: São Paulo Research Foundation (FAPESP)

Role: Coordinator

This project investigates Automated Machine Learning methods for continuous data streams, focusing on classification under non-stationary environments. It addresses challenges such as concept drift, concept evolution, novelty detection, and the automatic design and tuning of learning pipelines for streaming scenarios, where data must be processed in a single pass and cannot be stored in full.

National Institute of Artificial Intelligence for Social Good (IAPROBEM)

Period: 2025 - Present

Status: Ongoing

Role: Team Member

This initiative investigates how Artificial Intelligence can be applied to major national challenges with social impact. The project focuses on AI methods and tools for disaster prevention, environmental protection, health, citizenship, human rights, and social well-being, bringing together researchers from multiple regions of Brazil and from different disciplinary backgrounds.

Neuro-GEMA: A Grammar-based Evolutionary Method to Automatically Design Flexible Convolutional Neural Networks

Period: 2023 - 2025

Status: Completed

Funding: São Paulo Research Foundation (FAPESP)

Role: Coordinator

Neuro-GEMA explored grammar-based multi-objective evolutionary algorithms for Neural Architecture Search (NAS), with emphasis on the automatic design of convolutional neural networks. By combining grammatical representations and multi-objective optimization, the project sought models with strong predictive performance and favorable trade-offs in metrics such as model size. The resulting architectures were intended for smart-city applications, including urban monitoring and public-health related detection tasks.

Automatic Construction and Selection of Machine Learning Algorithms

Period: 2022 - 2024

Status: Completed

Funding: São Paulo Research Foundation (FAPESP) and FACEPE

Role: Coordinator

This project advanced research on meta-learning and automated algorithm design for classification. It investigated search-based approaches for automatically building learning algorithms, including decision trees, rule induction systems, Bayesian networks, deep neural networks, similarity functions for semi-supervised learning, classifier ensembles, and multi-target predictors. It also examined comparative aspects between algorithm selection, configuration, and automatic construction approaches.

IARA - Artificial Intelligence Recreating Environments

Period: 2022 - Present

Status: Ongoing

Funding: São Paulo Research Foundation (FAPESP)

Role: Team Member

IARA is an Applied Research Center in Artificial Intelligence organized as a national network of research nodes across Brazil. It brings together researchers and partner institutions to advance AI for smart cities, with activities spanning cybersecurity, education, infrastructure, environment, and health. The initiative emphasizes technology transfer, education, outreach, and the ethical, transparent, and effective use of AI in urban environments.

National Institute of Artificial Intelligence (IAIA)

Period: 2022 - Present

Status: Ongoing

Funding: CNPq INCT Program

Role: Team Member

This National Institute of Science and Technology in Artificial Intelligence was created to foster collaborative research, innovation, and human resource development in AI in Brazil. The initiative aims to strengthen the international visibility of Brazilian AI research while promoting advanced training in both foundational and applied Artificial Intelligence.
