Funded and collaborative research

Research projects

GPAM participates in funded projects and large-scale collaborative initiatives in Automated Machine Learning, data streams, evolutionary computation, neural architecture search, and socially impactful Artificial Intelligence.

AutoML for Data Streams

Period: 2026–Present

Status: Ongoing

Funding agency: FAPESP

Role: Coordinator

Summary: This project investigates Automated Machine Learning methods for continuous data streams, with emphasis on classification in non-stationary environments. It addresses challenges such as concept drift, concept evolution, novelty detection, and the automatic design and configuration of learning methods for streaming scenarios, where data must be processed in a single pass and cannot be fully stored in memory.

National Institute of Artificial Intelligence for Social Good (IAPROBEM)

Period: 2025–Present

Status: Ongoing

Role: Team member

Summary: This initiative investigates how Artificial Intelligence can be used to address major national challenges with social impact. The project focuses on AI methods and tools for disaster prevention, environmental protection, health, citizenship, human rights, and social well-being, bringing together researchers from multiple regions of Brazil and from different disciplinary backgrounds.

Neuro-GEMA: A Grammar-based Evolutionary Method to Automatically Design Flexible Convolutional Neural Networks

Period: 2023–2025

Status: Completed

Funding agency: FAPESP

Role: Coordinator

Summary: Neuro-GEMA explored grammar-based multi-objective evolutionary algorithms for Neural Architecture Search, with emphasis on the automatic design of convolutional neural networks. By combining grammatical representations and multi-objective optimization, the project sought architectures with strong predictive performance and favorable trade-offs in metrics such as model size, targeting applications related to smart cities and urban monitoring.

Automatic Construction and Selection of Machine Learning Algorithms

Period: 2022–2024

Status: Completed

Funding agency: FAPESP and FACEPE

Role: Coordinator

Summary: This project advanced research on meta-learning and automated algorithm design for classification. It investigated search-based approaches for automatically building learning algorithms, including decision trees, rule induction systems, Bayesian networks, deep neural networks, similarity functions for semi-supervised learning, classifier ensembles, and multi-target predictors, while also examining the relationship between algorithm selection, configuration, and construction.

IARA — Artificial Intelligence Recreating Environments

Period: 2022–Present

Status: Ongoing

Funding agency: FAPESP

Role: Team member

Summary: IARA is an Applied Research Center in Artificial Intelligence organized as a national network of research nodes across Brazil. The initiative brings together researchers and partner institutions to advance AI for smart cities in areas such as cybersecurity, education, infrastructure, environment, and health, with strong emphasis on technology transfer, education, outreach, and the ethical and effective use of AI.

National Institute of Artificial Intelligence (IAIA)

Period: 2022–Present

Status: Ongoing

Funding agency: CNPq INCT Program

Role: Team member

Summary: This National Institute of Science and Technology in Artificial Intelligence was created to foster collaborative research, innovation, and human resource development in AI in Brazil. The initiative aims to strengthen the international visibility of Brazilian AI research while promoting advanced training in both foundational and applied Artificial Intelligence.
