Research areas

Scientific agenda

GPAM investigates machine learning methods that are robust, adaptive, and computationally effective, with a balance between methodological innovation and practical applicability.

Automated Machine Learning (AutoML)

Methods for automatic model selection, hyperparameter optimization, pipeline design, and adaptive decision-making with reduced dependence on manual trial-and-error.

Evolutionary Computation

Search and optimization strategies based on evolutionary principles, including grammar-based methods, representation learning, and automated model construction.

Graph-based Learning

Construction and analysis of graphs for machine learning, especially in semi-supervised settings, transductive inference, and similarity-based representations.

Semi-Supervised and Weakly Supervised Learning

Approaches for extracting value from limited labels, partially annotated data, and scenarios in which supervision is scarce or expensive.

Data Streams and Adaptive Systems

Learning in non-stationary environments, including evolving data distributions, concept drift, incremental learning, and continuous evaluation.

Applied Predictive Modeling

Design and evaluation of predictive methods for structured data, time series, and application domains that require robustness, interpretability, and empirical validation.

Methodological values

How we approach research

  • Comparative experiments with strong baselines.
  • Attention to reproducibility and transparent reporting.
  • Evaluation across multiple datasets, scenarios, or operating conditions.
  • Integration of theoretical foundations and practical performance.
